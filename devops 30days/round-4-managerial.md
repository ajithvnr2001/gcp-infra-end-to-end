# Round 4 - Managerial / Final Round

## Goal

Show ownership, communication, learning ability, and production mindset.

## Questions

### 1. How do you handle production pressure?

```text
I focus on impact first. I communicate status clearly, avoid random changes, rollback if needed, and use telemetry to find root cause.
```

### 2. How do you prioritize tasks?

```text
Production impact and customer-facing issues first, then security/reliability risks, then planned improvements.
```

### 3. How do you work with developers?

```text
I use evidence from logs, metrics, CI results, and traces. I avoid blame and focus on restoring service and preventing recurrence.
```

### 4. What if you do not know a tool?

```text
I map it to concepts I know, read official docs, build a small lab, and validate with a practical task.
```

### 5. What would you improve in your project?

```text
Canary deployments, stronger image scanning, SLO alerts, policy-as-code, better integration tests, secret rotation, and AWS hands-on implementation.
```

### 6. How do you prevent repeated incidents?

```text
Postmortem, action items, better alerts, runbooks, tests, automation, and ownership.
```

### 7. Are you comfortable with on-call?

```text
Yes, if there are clear runbooks, alert quality, escalation paths, and post-incident improvement culture.
```

### 8. How do you learn AWS quickly?

```text
I map GCP services to AWS equivalents, then focus on AWS-specific differences like IAM roles, VPC route tables, security groups, and service integrations.
```

### 9. What kind of team do you want?

```text
A team that values automation, reliability, learning, clear ownership, and practical engineering.
```

### 10. Final pitch.

```text
I can contribute as a DevOps/Cloud Engineer because I understand the full delivery path: code, CI/CD, containers, Kubernetes, Terraform, cloud infrastructure, observability, incidents, and security. I also have the discipline to learn AWS quickly from my GCP foundation.
```
